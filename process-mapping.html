Process Mapping

Visualizing Workflows to Drive Understanding and Improvement

Why Process Mapping Matters

Process mapping transforms abstract workflows into visual documentation that everyone can understand. It's an essential tool for identifying inefficiencies, training new team members, ensuring consistency, and facilitating continuous improvement.

I specialize in creating clear, actionable process maps that serve as both documentation and roadmaps for optimization.

My Process Mapping Methodology

1

Discovery & Documentation

  • Interview stakeholders and process owners
  • Observe the process in action
  • Document all steps, decision points, and handoffs
  • Identify pain points and bottlenecks
2

Current State Mapping

  • Create visual representation of existing process
  • Identify roles and responsibilities
  • Document cycle times and wait periods
  • Highlight areas of waste or duplication
3

Analysis & Optimization

  • Identify improvement opportunities
  • Prioritize based on impact and feasibility
  • Design future state process
  • Estimate expected improvements
4

Future State Mapping

  • Create optimized process map
  • Define success metrics
  • Develop implementation plan
  • Create training materials
5

Implementation & Monitoring

  • Roll out new process
  • Train team members
  • Monitor performance metrics
  • Iterate and refine as needed

Types of Process Maps I Create

Flowcharts

Traditional flowcharts showing sequential steps, decision points, and process flow. Best for straightforward processes with clear decision logic.

Use Cases: Approval workflows, troubleshooting guides, decision trees

Swimlane Diagrams

Process maps organized by role or department, showing handoffs and responsibilities. Excellent for identifying silos and communication gaps.

Use Cases: Cross-functional processes, role clarification, handoff documentation

Value Stream Maps

Detailed analysis of process steps with cycle times, wait times, and value-add identification. Great for lean improvement initiatives.

Use Cases: Manufacturing processes, service delivery, time optimization

SIPOC Diagrams

High-level view showing Suppliers, Inputs, Process, Outputs, and Customers. Perfect for initial process understanding.

Use Cases: Process overview, stakeholder alignment, scope definition

Process Mapping Best Practices

Creating Effective Process Maps

Do:

  • Use consistent symbols and notation
  • Keep it simple and clear
  • Include all stakeholders in validation
  • Document assumptions and exceptions
  • Update maps when processes change
  • Make maps accessible to team

Avoid:

  • Making maps too detailed or complex
  • Skipping exception handling
  • Creating maps in isolation
  • Using maps as one-time exercise
  • Ignoring informal workarounds
  • Letting maps become outdated
